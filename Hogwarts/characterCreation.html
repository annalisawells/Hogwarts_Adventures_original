Attributes

These reflect your characters quantified innate physical and mental abilities. Each starting attribute has a score that ranges from 2 (miserably weak) to 12 (amazing).
    Choose TWO as your favored attributes.
  • Strength: your ability to exert force and do damage physically
  • Dexterity: coordination, dexterousness, and deftness, such as your speed and reflexes: your ability to avoid being hit by a Bludger and like dangers.
  • Consitution: vigour, stamina, and health; including, but not limited to your toughness, your ability to withstand pain, to throw off the effects of poison or sickness, to resist heat or cold, etc.
  • Intelligence: your character's memory, capacity for clear and reasoned thought, intelligence, and store of basic knowledge
  • Wisdom: your strength of will: your ability to stave off fear and domination and to remain true to yourself and your word, regardless of blandishments or torture; also: your common sense and insight: your ability to determine which is true, or the best course of action, or what others feel or believe
  • Charisma: your character's force of personality, presence, and ability to inspire feelings such as awe, fear, or courage
  • **Magic:** the ability to cast spells properly, whether nautally gifted or through hard work and studying intently. Note: Magic is already a bonus favored class for this campaign!

How to calculate your attribute scores: RANDOM: Roll 4d6, discard the lowest die result, and add the three remaining results together. Record this total and repeat the process until six numbers are generated. Assign these totals to your ability scores as you see fit (typically, the highest two will go towards your favored attributes)

In the case that you try this random mentod and it gives you very sad scores, feel free to use the BUY method instead: Start with a 10 for each ability score, and distribute 12 points (1-to-1 ratio, so if you add 2 points to Dexterity, you will have a Dex score of 12 and have a remaining 10 other points to spend) amongst them as you see fit. Don't bother to save any, as you cannot use them later!

Heritage

Muggle-born

Your character was born to parents who were both non-magic folk. "Mudblood" (meaning 'dirty blood') is a highly insulting term used towards those who are born of Muggle decent.

Examples:

Hermione Granger, Colin Creevey

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge (Muggle), Use Muggle device
  • -2 penalty to Use Magic device
  • +2 Intelligence, +1 Magic, -2 Magic
  • Bonus Edge: Magic Talent

Half-Muggle

One of your character's parents was a wizard and the other was not or in your family's past someone was of nonmagic blood.

Examples:

Seamus Finnigan, Harry Potter

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge (Muggle), Use Muggle device
    -2 penalty to Knowledge (Magic), Use Magic device
    OR
    +2 bonus to Knowledge (Magic), Use Magic device
    -2 penalty to Knowledge (Muggle), Use Muggle device

  • +2 Wisdom, +1 Charisma, -2 Strength
  • Bonus Edge: Magic Talent

Pure-blood

Your character comes from one of the great wizard families, not a drop of Muggle blood in your veins.

Examples:

Draco Malfoy, Ron Weasley

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge (Magic), Use Magic device
  • -2 penalty to Knowledge (Muggle), Use Muggle device
  • +2 Magic, +1 Charisma, -2 Dexterity
  • Bonus Edge: Magic Talent

Wizard House

Gryffindor

Those who have been chosen to be a part of Gryffindor House have such qualities that Godric, himself, embodied: honor, courage, and forthrightness. If you are of this house, you are be expected to be trustworthy and brave, but know when to break the rules when it's necessary to do what is right.

Edges (Choose one):

Bold, Courageous, Forthright, Valour, Valiant

Skill Picks:

Acrobatics +2, Athletics +1, Diplomacy +1, Bluff +1, Spellcraft +1, Sleight of Hand +1

Hufflepuff

Those who have been chosen to join the Hufflepuff House are renowned for their hardy work ethic and steadfastness. A Hufflepuff remains true and loyal to the end.

Edges (Choose one):

Doughty, Faithful, Fortitude, Indomitable, Valiant

Skill Picks:

Diplomacy +2, Handle Animal +1, Investigate +1, First Aid +1, Perception +1, Spellcraft +1

Slytherin

The snake isn't the symbol of the Slytherin House just because its founder; it is the embodiment of what thosechosen in the house stand for: cunning, guile, and always ready to strike when an opportunity presents itself. Scheming in dark corners and bullying others is just a taste of what makes up a typical Slytherin

Edges (Choose one):

Bold, Competitive, Confident, Keen-Eared, Keen-Eyed

Skill Picks:

Intimidate +2, Forgery +1, Sense Motive +1, Sleight of Hand +1, Spellcraft +1, Stealth +1

Ravenclaw

Students who are intelligent, studious, and resourceful generally are found in the house of Ravenclaw. A quick witand the timely spell are the signatures of a member of the Ravenclaw House.

Edges (Choose one):

Concentration, Curious, Meticulous, Studious, Wise

Skill Picks:

Spellcraft +2, Brew Potion or Divination +1, Appraise +1, Knowledge (magic) +1, Knowledge (muggle) +1
