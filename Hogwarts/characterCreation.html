Attributes

These reflect your characters quantified innate physical and mental abilities. Each starting attribute has a score that ranges from 2 (miserably weak) to 12 (amazing).

Primary:

    Choose TWO as your favored attributes.
  • Strength: your ability to exert force and do damage physically
  • Vitality: vigour, stamina, and health
  • Nimbleness: coordination, dexterousness, and deftness
  • Perception: your awareness of your surroundings, including your ability to spot that which is hidden
  • Bearing: your character's force of personality, presence, and ability to inspire feelings such as awe, fear, or courage
  • Intellect: your character's memory, capacity for clear and reasoned thought, intelligence, and store of basic knowledge
  • Masgic: the ability to cast spells properly

Secondary:

    Choose ONE as your favored attribute.
  • Stamina: your toughness, your ability to withstand pain, to throw off the effects of poison or sickness, to resist heat or cold, etc.
  • Swiftness: your speed and reflexes: your ability to avoid being hit by a Bludger and like dangers.
  • Willpower: your strength of will: your ability to stave off fear and domination and to remain true to yourself and your word, regardless of blandishments or torture.
  • Wisdom: your common sense and insight: your ability to determine which is true, or the best course of action, or what others feel or believe
  • Defense: your natural ability to avoid blows and weapon fire in combat. Your Defense rating equals 7 + Nimbleness modifier.

Heritage

Muggle-born

Your character was born to parents who were both non-magic folk. "Mudblood" (meaning 'dirty blood') is a highly insulting term used towards those who are born of Muggle decent.

Examples:

Hermione Granger, Colin Creevey

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge, Culture: (Native Muggle) skill tests
  • -2 penalty to Use Item: Magic skill tests
  • Bonus Edge: Magic Talent

Half-Muggle

One of your character's parents was a wizard and the other was not or in your family's past someone was of nonmagic blood.

Examples:

Seamus Finnigan, Harry Potter

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Use Item: Muggle skill tests AND
    -2 penalty to Use Item: Magic skill tests
    OR
    +2 bonus to Use Item: Magic skill tests
    -2 penalty to Use Item: Muggle skill tests

  • Bonus Edge: Magic Talent

Pure-blood

Your character comes from one of the great wizard families, not a drop of Muggle blood in your veins.

Examples:

Draco Malfoy, Ron Weasley

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Use Item: Magic skill tests
  • -2 penalty to Use Item: Muggle skill tests
  • Bonus Edge: Magic Talent
