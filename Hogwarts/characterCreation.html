#1 Attributes

These reflect your characters quantified innate physical and mental abilities. Each starting attribute has a score. 10 is considered average, and the higher you go the cooler you are!
  • Strength: your ability to exert force and do damage physically
  • Dexterity: coordination, dexterousness, and deftness, such as your speed and reflexes: your ability to avoid being hit by a Bludger and like dangers.
  • Consitution: vigor, stamina, and health; including, but not limited to your toughness, your ability to withstand pain, to throw off the effects of poison or sickness, to resist heat or cold, etc.
  • Intelligence: your character's memory, capacity for clear and reasoned thought, intelligence, and store of basic knowledge
  • Wisdom: your strength of will: your ability to stave off fear and domination and to remain true to yourself and your word, regardless of blandishments or torture; also: your common sense and insight: your ability to determine which is true, or the best course of action, or what others feel or believe
  • Charisma: your character's force of personality, presence, and ability to inspire feelings such as awe, fear, or courage
  • **Magic:** the ability to cast spells properly, whether nautally gifted or through hard work and studying intently.

How to calculate your attribute scores: RANDOM: Roll 4d6, discard the lowest die result, and add the three remaining results together. Record this total and repeat the process until SEVEN numbers are generated. Assign these totals to your ability scores as you see fit (typically, the highest two will go towards your favored attributes)

In the case that you try this random method and it gives you very sad scores, feel free to use the BUY method instead: Start with a 10 for each ability score, and distribute 12 points (1-to-1 ratio, so if you add 2 points to Dexterity, you will have a Dex score of 12 and have a remaining 10 other points to spend) amongst them as you see fit. Don't bother to save any, as you cannot use them later!

Note on skills:

All of the bonus skill points granted by your Heritage and Wizard House Choice will be input to the Skills "Misc" column. You cannot have more ranks in a skill than your current character level, but the Misc column is not limited!

Any bonus skill points that are awarded go into the "Misc" column

#2 Heritage

Muggle-born

Your character was born to parents who were both non-magic folk. "Mudblood" (meaning 'dirty blood') is a highly insulting term used towards those who are born of Muggle decent.

Examples:

Hermione Granger, Colin Creevey

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge (Muggle), +2 Use Muggle device
  • -2 penalty to Use Magic device
  • +2 Intelligence, +1 Dexterity, -2 Magic

Half-Muggle

One of your character's parents was a wizard and the other was not or in your family's past someone was of nonmagic blood.

Examples:

Seamus Finnigan, Harry Potter

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge (Muggle), +1 Use Muggle device
    -2 penalty to Knowledge (Magic), -1 Use Magic device
    OR
    +2 bonus to Knowledge (Magic), +1 Use Magic device
    -2 penalty to Knowledge (Muggle), -1 Use Muggle device

  • +2 Wisdom, +1 Charisma, -2 Strength

Pure-blood

Your character comes from one of the great wizard families, not a drop of Muggle blood in your veins.

Examples:

Draco Malfoy, Ron Weasley

Qualities:

  • +2 bonus to Knowledge (Magic), +2 Use Magic device
  • -2 penalty to Knowledge (Muggle), -2 Use Muggle device
  • +2 Magic, +1 Charisma, -2 Dexterity

#3 Wizard House

Gryffindor

Those who have been chosen to be a part of Gryffindor House have such qualities that Godric, himself, embodied: honor, courage, and forthrightness. If you are of this house, you are be expected to be trustworthy and brave, but know when to break the rules when it's necessary to do what is right.

Skill Picks:

Acrobatics +2, Athletics +1, Diplomacy +1, Bluff +1, Spellcraft +1, Sleight of Hand +1

Hufflepuff

Those who have been chosen to join the Hufflepuff House are renowned for their hardy work ethic and steadfastness. A Hufflepuff remains true and loyal to the end.

Skill Picks:

Diplomacy +2, Handle Animal +1, Investigate +1, First Aid +1, Perception +1, Spellcraft +1

Slytherin

The snake isn't the symbol of the Slytherin House just because its founder; it is the embodiment of what those chosen in the house stand for: cunning, guile, and always ready to strike when an opportunity presents itself. Scheming in dark corners and bullying others is just a taste of what makes up a typical Slytherin

Skill Picks:

Intimidate +2, Forgery +1, Sense Motive +1, Sleight of Hand +1, Spellcraft +1, Stealth +1

Ravenclaw

Students who are intelligent, studious, and resourceful generally are found in the house of Ravenclaw. A quick wit and the timely spell are the signatures of a member of the Ravenclaw House.

Skill Picks:

Spellcraft +2, Brew Potion or Divination +1, Appraise +1, Knowledge (magic) +1, Knowledge (muggle) +1

#4 Skills

Skills represent some of the most basic and yet most fundamental abilities your character possesses. As your character advances in level, he can gain new skills and improve his existing skills. Note: The extra skill points granted by your Heritage and Wizard House are bonus and do not count towards your 4 +Int mod.

  • Acrobatics (Dex)
  • Athletics (Str)
  • Appraise (Wis)
  • Bluff (Cha)
  • Brew Potion* (Magic)
  • Diplomacy (Cha)
  • Disable Device* (Dex)
  • Disguise (Cha)
  • Divination* (Magic)
  • Escape Artist* (Dex)
  • First Aid (Wis)
  • Fly (Magic)
  • Forgery* (Int)
  • Handle Animal* (Cha)
  • Intimidate (Cha)
  • Investigate (Wis)
  • Knowledge (magic)* (Int)
  • Knowledge (muggle)* (Int)
  • Perception (Wis)
  • Sense Motive (Wis)
  • Sleight of Hand* (Dex)
  • Spellcraft* (Magic)
  • Stealth (Dex)
  • Use Magic Device* (Int)
  • Use Muggle Device* (Int)
